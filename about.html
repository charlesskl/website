Our Story

38 years of
craftsmanship.

From a single workshop in Sha Tau Kok, Hong Kong, Royal Regent has grown into a multi-site OEM and ODM toy manufacturer trusted by leading global brands — delivering safe, compliant, and precision-engineered toys from China and Indonesia to markets worldwide.

We believe great manufacturing is built on trust, not just transactions. For 38 years, we have partnered with brands to turn creative visions into finished products — with the craftsmanship, compliance, and consistency their markets demand.

Our History

Nearly four decades
of continuous growth.

1987

Founded in Sha Tau Kok

Royal Regent was established in Sha Tau Kok, Hong Kong — beginning as a focused plastic toy manufacturer. From the outset, the company built the quality systems, client relationships, and manufacturing discipline that continue to define it today.

2000

Entering Plush & Soft Toys

Royal Regent expanded beyond plastic manufacturing by launching dedicated plush and soft toy production lines. This milestone opened entirely new product categories and allowed the company to serve a broader range of global brand partners.

2010

Doll Lines Integrated

Doll manufacturing was fully integrated, combining precision plastic molding with in-house sewing craftsmanship. The result was a complete production capability for fashion dolls and baby dolls — face painting, hair rooting, outfit assembly — all under one roof.

2016

Remote Control Vehicles

Royal Regent entered RC vehicle manufacturing — the company's most technically demanding category. This expansion required advanced electronics integration, structural engineering expertise, and compliance with FCC, CE, and ASTM F963 safety standards.

2023

Indonesia Phase I

A landmark year: Royal Regent established its first Southeast Asian manufacturing facility in Indonesia. Phase I marked a strategic step in supply chain diversification — reducing concentration risk and positioning the company closer to key international markets.

2025

Indonesia Phase II

Indonesia Phase II commenced, scaling production capacity and deepening Royal Regent's Southeast Asian footprint. This expansion strengthens our ability to serve international customers with faster lead times, greater flexibility, and a more resilient global supply chain.

Global Operations

Four sites.
One integrated supply chain.

01

Dongguan, China

Our flagship facility and operational headquarters. Home to in-house mold development, injection molding, and plastic figure production — the core of our manufacturing capability since our early years.

02

Heyuan, China

Dedicated to plush and soft toy production. This facility handles material sourcing, pattern cutting, sewing, stuffing, and final quality inspection for all plush and character soft toy lines.

03

Hunan, China

Specialising in doll and costume production. The Hunan site brings together skilled garment and assembly workers to produce fashion dolls, baby dolls, and dress-up costume ranges.

04

Indonesia

Our newest and growing facility, established in 2023 with Phase I and expanded through Phase II in 2025. A strategic asset for supply chain diversification and proximity to key international markets.

What We Stand For

Principles that guide
everything we manufacture.

Quality

Built-in, not bolted-on

Every product we manufacture is produced to ASTM F963 (US) and EN 71 (EU) safety standards. Quality is embedded at every stage — from material selection and mold design through assembly and final inspection — not added as an afterthought.

Partnership

Long-term, not transactional

We work as an extension of our clients' teams — providing OEM and ODM services that go beyond manufacturing. From concept development and sampling to production planning and export logistics, we are a partner at every stage of the product journey.

Compliance

Every market, every standard

Navigating safety regulations across the US, EU, and other markets is complex. Royal Regent maintains rigorous compliance processes across all manufacturing sites to ensure every product meets the regulatory requirements of its destination market.
